This is a list of top 8 sad moments from the stories, The Crucible, and The Great Gatsby, which we have read in Mr. Wright's English class. These stories have many sad moments that show the audience various aspects of the story. To me, the sad moments in a story are the most engaging moments of the story. They engage me, the reader, in the story and make a sense of connection between me and the characters in the story.

Without further ado, here are the top 8 saddest moments in the stories that we have read in Mr. Wright’s English class!

8. The death of Giles Corey

Giles Corey was John Proctor's friend and a person who supported his idea. When he was being interogated by the court, he was refusing to answer any of the questions that they asked him. They decided to get the answer's out of him by force. They put heavy bolders on his body, but he still never gave them the answers. This did not stop them, they put more weights on him until he died.

7. Gatsby takes the blame for the death of Myrtle

After the confrontation between Tom and Gatsby, Daisy gets very emotional and wants to be the one who drives back home. She is driving way over the speed limit and hits a women. It turns out that the woman she hit was Myrtle. Gatsby takes the blame for her death because he loves Daisy that much. He does not want her to suffer the servere consequences of her actions.

6. Reunion of Gatsby and Daisy

While this moment was is not sad like the others, it makes you cry out of happiness for Gatsby and Daisy. It had been 5 years since they had last met and they were finally getting to together again! Gatsby had big plans for him and Daisy. He would show Daisy how he had changed over the 5 years from just a regular man to one of the most richest people in New York.

5. Jordan tells Nick about Tom's Affairs

While Nick was at Tom's house in chapter one, he notices that Tom is getting many phone calls. That's when Jordan tells Nick that Tom is having an affair with someone else. Later, we also hear that he was not present while Daisy was giving birth to her child. Daisy is very upset about this, but she say this to Tom. Tom is also noticeably very racist and talks about how white people are the superior race while eating dinner with Nick.

4. The fight between Gatsby and Tom

This was the moment in the story where everything starts to fall apart. Daisy and Gatsby confront Tom about the affair between them. After they tell him, he doesn't seem to be very upset, instead he starts to tell everyone what kind of man Gatsby truely is. While Tom was saying this, Daisy is starting to get very confused and starts to think if she should have had an affair with Gatsby in the first place.

3. The Death Of Myrtle

After the confrontation, Daisy wanted to drive back home to let her emotions out. She was driving very fast and recklessly. While she was driving, Myrtle jumps out on the road infront of Daisy's car and Murtle dies. After this, the tensions between Gatsby and Daisy increases even more. When Tom find out what happens he seems to be very sad. It is implied in the story that Tom was having an affair with Myrtle.

2. The death of John Proctor

John was accused of witchcraft by Mary Warren. She was making it up because she wanted to protect herself from Abigail. She knew that if she lied, she would be protected just like Abigail and the others. After sometime, John Proctor was sentenced to execution. He was executed along with 2 other women who were accused of being witches in front of the whole village.

1. The Death Of Gatsby

After Murtle died, her husband was very sad and depressed. That sadness and depression eventually turned into anger when he found out that Gatsby was the person who was driving the car, even though Gatsby was not the one driving. So one day he took his gun, and went to Gatsby's house. While Gatsby was in his pool, he got shot and passed away. After that, Murtle's husband points the gun towards himself, and shoots a second time.
