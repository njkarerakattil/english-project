This is a list of top 8 sad moments from the stories, The Crucible, and The Great Gatsby, which we have read in Mr. Wright's English class. These stories have many sad moments that show the audience various aspects of the story. To me, the sad moments in a story are the most engaging moments of the story. They engage me, the reader, in the story and make a sense of connection between me and the characters in the story.

Without further ado, here are the top 8 saddest moments in the stories that we have read in Mr. Wright’s English class!

8. The death of Giles Corey

Giles Corey was John Proctor's friend and a person who supported his idea. When he was being interogated by the court, he was refusing to answer any of the questions that they asked him. They decided to get the answer's out of him by force. They put heavy bolders on his body, but he still never gave them the answers. This did not stop them, they put more weights on him until he died.

7. Gatsby takes the blame for the death of Myrtle

After the confrontation between Tom and Gatsby, Daisy gets very emotional and wants to be the one who drives back home. She is driving way over the speed limit and hits a women. It turns out that the woman she hit was Myrtle. Gatsby takes the blame for her death because he loves Daisy that much. He does not want her to suffer the servere consequences of her actions.
